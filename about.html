About the Conference

Mission Statement

To launch a nationwide Movement for creating Ideal Villages which uplift and empower underserved communities through the adoption of Best Practices in Rural Development and appropriate Technology. The Ideal Village concept focuses on enabling essential need for underserved and rural communities globally, Through holistic and sustainable technology solutions, By promoting collaboration among stakeholders and all organizations working on social impact.

7th Ideal Village Conference

7th Ideal Village Conference will be held in India in view of the Indian Government’s major push in rural development. India’s target GDP growth rate of 8.5% per annum will not be possible without a healthy rural economy.This is to initiate a global movement in Integrated Rural Development . Given the high quality and diversity of topics, speakers, organizations and participants over the past six years, the theme for 2022 Ideal Village Conference will be “Rural Transformation for Sustainable Growth” to capture the lessons learnt and best practices from successful implementations and thereby define the future of global rural development.Best Practices will be chosen in each of the 6 tracks, namely, Agriculture and Farming, Water and Sanitation, Healthcare and Nutrition, Education and Skilling, Energy and Environment, Livelihoods and Sustainability through a global competition to be completed in the next 7 months. The three top best practices in each of the 6 tracks will be featured at the 2022 Conference to be held at the Banaras Hindu University (BHU), Varanasi, India.Corporate sponsors will be invited to support each of the 18 Best Practice (BP) candidates under their respective CSR (Corporate Social Responsibility) mandates. In addition, these BP solutions will be recommended to CSR programs, State and Central Government agencies, Foundations, for financial support and scaling across the nation.
Criteria for Best Practice selection will include for example, Scaling potential, Implementation success, Impact metrics, Technology application/innovation, Collaboration levels, Stakeholder involvement, Sustainability, Unintended consequences (if any), Quality of funding, Holistic solution value and related factors.

Speaker Framework

  • Keynote addresses by Global leaders: PMO, UN SDG, etc (30,000ft view)

  • Invited speakers to address various points-of view (15,000ft view):

    • Central government priorities - Ministry of Rural Development & Panchayati Raj

    • State Government - State Chief Ministers

    • Local Governments - Panchayat Members panel (Mission Samriddhi led)

    • Academic/Research Institute: IITs, Stanford (Directors panel)

    • Industry CSR initiative: CSR Panel

  • Speakers from practitioner (Ground level view)

    • NGOs from each field (Parallel sessions): From a global competition of Best Practices, winners in each of the Tracks will be invited to present in their respective sessions:

      • Agriculture & Farming

      • Water & Sanitation

      • Health & Nutrition

      • Energy & Environment

      • Education & Skilling

      • Livelihood & Sustainability

    • Best Practices Awards - Special VIP Invitees (Corporate / Govt )

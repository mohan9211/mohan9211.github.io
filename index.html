Background to the Conference

Almost two-thirds of the world’s population lives in rural areas, the vast majority with poor access to the basic essentials of life, namely,clean water, access to healthcare, education, energy, and sustaina livelihoods.

While numerous non-profits, foundations and Government programs have focused on improving rural lives, these efforts were often “silo’d” or disconnected with the result that hundreds of millions still lack these basic needs. Tragically, their plight is worsening today due to the compounded effects of the Covid pandemic, major regional conflicts, supply chain disruption and inflation.

The annual Ideal Village Conference series was launched in 2016 at Stanford University to serve as a collaboration platform for diverse organizations involved in rural development to connect and evolve a holistic approach which can help improve their collective impact on rural lives.

The six Conferences since 2016 have documented a wealth of learnings and experiences from rural development efforts, covering challenges, solutions, best practice examples and project reviews, all of which may be found at: https://www.scienceforsociety.com .

The 7th in this series of Conferences will be held at Banaras Hindu University (BHU), thus coming to India for the first time. Given the past learnings, diverse global experiences, and success stories, the theme for the 2022 Conference at BHU is Sustainable Growth through Rural Transformation.

Collaboration among various stakeholders, NGOs, Corporate entities, Government agencies, Universities, Civil Society, Media, and Foundations involved in rural development has been promoted through an annual Conference held at Stanford University since 2016, as well as regular webinars and NGO activities organized by the WHEELS Global Foundation

Mission and Goal

Our mission is to launch a nationwide Movement for creating Ideal Villages which uplift and empower underserved communities through the adoption of Best Practices in Rural Development with appropriate Technology

Our goal is to collectively impact 20% of India’s rural population through improved access to Water, Health, Education, Energy, and Livelihoods in a Sustainable way, and by leveraging technology at scale.

One of the key pillars for development in the next decade would be transforming rural communities by applying appropriate technologies to become the engines of global economic growth and create future markets

Why Participate?

With more than 850 million people live in rural or rurban areas, India can become a US$5 Trillion economy primarily through rural development. This represents a great opportunity to find new markets, develop new labor sources, enhance manufacturing, with large demands for affordable housing, healthcare, energy, education, etc.

With 100 years since Indian independence in 2047, India must make the investments to build rural infrastructure, implement social impact projects, improve agriculture, and market many more goods and services to this neglected segment.

Rural youth are highly motivated to improve their lives and reverse their migration patterns to live in their communities by birth and start entrepreneurial ventures.

This conference will raise the awareness of rural development opportunities and bring industry, academia, NGOs, and Government together to exchange ideas for scaling rural technology applications.

Learn from distinguished speakers, panel discussants, exhibitors, and poster presenters about the ‘best practices’ for implementing water conservation and management, scalable healthcare initiatives such as Telemedicine, emerging technologies for alternative energy, and ‘collaborative governance’ of public-private-partnerships’ by including grass roots Panchayats and Village Groups.

Opportunities to meet senior government leaders, corporate executives and other experts in the field, discuss various challenges for development

Who should attend?

  • Government: Policy Leaders, Department Secretaries, Project Leaders and staff involved in rural development missions covering water, health, education, energy, livelihoods, etc.
  • Industry: Senior executives of companies that make and/or sell products and services in development projects for infrastructure, manufacturing plants, education, energy, healthcare, and sustainable livelihoods. Distribution and retail organizations that source products from rural areas.
  • Banking and Finance: Micro-finance companies, Banks and financial institutions active in projects related to village communities and rural development.
  • Academia, Research Labs: enior academics or scientists from all disciplines who are working on rural technologies or currently have technologies that can be applied for such purposes.
  • Non-Profits or NGOs: Practitioners who wish to learn of successful implementations, network with others with similar interests, seek funding, and explore partnerships.

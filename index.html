Scopes/Overview/Best Practices

WGF Conference will be held in India in view of the Indian Government’s major push in rural development. India’s target GDP growth rate of 8.5% per annum will not be possible without a healthy rural economy.

This is to initiate a global movement in Integrated Rural Development. Given the high quality and diversity of topics, speakers, organizations and participants over the past six years, the theme for 2022 Ideal Village Conference will be “Rural Transformation for Sustainable Growth” to capture the lessons learnt and best practices from successful implementations and thereby define the future of global rural development.

Best Practices will be chosen in each of the 6 tracks, namely, Agriculture and Farming, Water and Sanitation, Healthcare and Nutrition, Education and Skilling, Energy and Environment, Livelihoods and Sustainability. A through a global competition to be completed in the next 7 months. The three top best practices in each of the 6 tracks will be featured at the 2022 Conference to be held at the Banaras Hindu University (BHU), Varanasi, India.

Corporate sponsors will be invited to support each of the 18 Best Practice (BP) candidates under their respective CSR (Corporate Social Responsibility) mandates. In addition, these BP solutions will be recommended to CSR programs, State and Central Government agencies, Foundations, for financial support and scaling across the nation.

Criteria for Best Practice selection will include for example, Scaling potential, Implementation success, Impact metrics, Technology application/innovation, Collaboration levels, Stakeholder involvement, Sustainability, Unintended consequences (if any), Quality of funding, Holistic solution value and related factors.

Goals/Expected Outcomes

Identify Best Practices in rural development for scaling Nationwide through CSR and Governmental support.

Define the foundation for a Rural Economy Framework which can accelerate GDP growth globally.

Create a Public-Private-Community-Partnership model for adopting programs, funding, measurable outcomes. Establish working groups of leaders from Government, Industry, Academia, and NGOs to track and report progress.

Establish an International Institute of Development(IID) which will help create Institutes for Rural Development(Ideal/SMART Village) in India and also at Stanford's new School for Sustainability;leading to a global network of such academic centers over the next 5-10 years.

Set up a collaborative Council for Rural Development in IITs, Regional Colleges, State and Community colleges to promote innovation and disseminate knowledge (eg. SVERI, Wardh, Unnat Bharat Abhiyan etc); jointly with relevant institutes like Stanford, Harvard, MIT, John Hopkins in USA and others as applicable.

Create/develop a National Database of rural development programs, skills, contacts similar to the European Network for Rural Development. Serve as foundation for Community Collaboration.

WFG & Ideal Village Program

WHEELS Global Foundation(WFG)and the Ideal Village program(a joint initiative of Science for Society and Stanford University School of Medicine), together with the Institute of Management Studies, Banaras Hindu University (BHU) are pleased to announce the upcoming 7th Annual Ideal Village Conference from November 4-6, 2022 in Varanasi, India. Held at Stanford University for the past 6 years, this annual forum is a one-of-a kind-conference focused on sustainable rural development, unique in its holistic scope and emphasis on collaboration among all stakeholders. Since 2016, the annual Ideal Village Conference at Stanford University has served as an integrated collaboration platform for NGOs, Corporate entities, Government agencies, Universities, and Foundations working on holistic solutions for water and sanitation, healthcare and nutrition, education, energy and environment, and sustainable livelihoods that lead to healthy, self- sustained, and economically viable rural communities globally.

WHEELS Global Foundation (WGF) is an initiative of the alumni of Indian Institutes of Technology (IIT), more than 250,000 strong world-wide, incorporated in 2013 at the urging of former President of India, the late Dr Abdul Kalam. WGF has been active in application of Technology in Rural Development in the areas of…

  • W ater
  • H ealth
  • E ducation
  • E nergy
  • L ivelihoods
  • S ustainability

… leading to the acronym WHEELS. Given the commonality of objectives and focus areas of the Ideal Village Program and WHEELS, this partnership has led to a synergistic and efficient model for delivering on the essential needs in rural development. The 1 st Ideal Village conference at Stanford University was held in June 2016, focused on Innovations for the Ideal Village, while subsequent years covered significant themes including Collaborations for the Ideal Village (2017), Empowerment of Women in Rural/Underserved Communities (2018), Corporate Social Responsibility (2019), Sustainability and the Pandemic (2020), and Post-Pandemic Normal – The Path Forward (2021). These conferences attracted attendees from over 35 countries and featured experts from over 75 leading organizations and multiple Stanford faculty members involved in a wide spectrum of rural development issues.

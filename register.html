Instructions

  1. Format Specifications

    • Use only Arial, black font color, and a Font size of 11 points or larger.
    • Use at least one (1.0) inch margins for all pages.
    • Consecutively number each page throughout the application.
    • A smaller Font size can be used for figures, graphs, tables and footnotes, but it must be readily legible.
    • Use English and avoid jargon. If terms are not universally known, spell out the term the first time it is used and note the appropriate abbreviation in parentheses. The abbreviation may be used thereafter in your document.

  2. Submission of the Best Practice Full Application

    Submit the following materials in one PDF file via the Competition Website. Collate all the application materials in this order:

    • Face Page (maximum 1 page – all sections must be completed)
    • Best Practice Application (maximum 3 pages, must include quantitative & qualitative data)
    • Appendix (Figures, Photographs, References; maximum 2 pages)
    • Budget required for Scaling & Sustainability (maximum 2 pages)
    • Supportive Documents (1 page each):
      • Registration Certificate (or Certificate of Incorporation)
      • Brief Bio-sketch of the Leadership team (3 max)
      • Funding sources over the past 5 years
      • Summary statement from independent program assessment (if available)

  3. Preparing your Best Practice Full Application

    • Face Page (maximum 1 page)
      1. Item. Title of Project
      2. Item. CEO/... (name of the applicant)
      3. Item. Primary Area of Focus (must correspond to ONE of the six (6) Global Competition Tracks – see above)
      4. Item. Collaborators & Stakeholders

        5. Names and Contact information (email & mobile phone) of three or more Collaborators involved in this project.
        Names and Contact information (email & mobile phone) of three or more Stakeholders in the villages being served

      5. Item. Approval of Local Government or State Government Agencies

        6. Names and Contact information (email & mobile phone) of the Official(s) who are involved / gave the approvals

      6. Item. Project Summary

        7. Do not exceed 200 words. Write a succinct and accurate description of the ongoing work. State the application's broad, long-term objectives and scaling potential. This section should be informative to others, such that a committee member who is not familiar with your field, will understand. Avoid describing past accomplishments.

      7. Item. Youtube Video introduction

        8. Prepare a 90-second video introduction highlighting your project and its impact on the village(s). Post your video on YouTube; copy and paste the web address / hyperlink over here.

    • Your Project Summary (maximum 3 pages)
      1. Specific Aims, Problem addressed

        2. List the broad, long-term objectives and the goal of your rural development/social impact project, e.g., what problem are you addressing & why this is important.

      2. Background, Significance, Description

        3. Briefly sketch the background, any prior knowledge or work done by others, and then specifically your differentiation, value add, uniqueness, innovation or any gaps/challenges that your project was able to bridge/resolve. Describe, list key highlights of your work scope.

      3. Project Implementation, methods, resources, collaborators, costs, funding

        4. In this section, provide an account of the project implementation - methodology, resources, technologies, tools, and scale or reach. Describe how stakeholders, collaborators, partners played a role in implementation. What were your costs (budgets vs actuals) and funding sources.

      4. Impact, success metrics, lessons learned, innovation>

        5. Describe outcome of the project, impact achieved (# of people affected, tangible results to show problem addressed), how measured, data collected and statistically analyzed, methodology for impact assessment, benchmarking if any. Describe any novel concepts, approaches, tools, or technologies that made a difference. What lessons were learned, which can be applied in future scaling; what were the key success factors. How does the outcome contribute towards a holistic solution for rural development.

      5. Future Steps, improvements, scaling and Sustainability

        6. Define follow-up work and your plans for scaling to impact more villages, expand to districts, State and eventually at the national level. What is the long-term sustainability, and how will this project impact rural economics as well as any environmental impacts

    • Appedix (maximum 2 pages)

      • You may include figures, algorithms, flow-charts, photographs, and references in the Appendix section.

    • Budget required for Scaling & Sustainability (maximum 1 pages)

      • Describe your scaling vision or plans - funds required to scale this project 10-100 fold from its current size and footprint (e.g., expanding from 5 villages to 50 to 500 villages covered) and for sustaining the expanded services for the next 5 years (January 2023 to December 2027). Do you see the potential for scaling to national level – share your vision

    • Supporting Documents
      1. Registration Certificate (or Certificate of Incorporation)
      2. Brief Biosketch of the Leadership
      3. Funding sources over the past 5 years
      4. Status of collaboration or ecosystem partnerships, funding sources
      5. Summary statement from independent program assessment (if available)

  4. The Review Process

    1. Overview

      2. The review process is modeled after the MacArthur Foundation’s 100&Change Program. It is intended to ensure that all entries are evaluated fairly, equitably, timely, and conducted in a manner free from bias. Each application will be assigned to two independent reviewers. Prize Winners will be notified on or before October 10, 2022.

    2. Scoring

      3. Reviewers will be instructed to evaluate each application by addressing the core review criteria listed below. For each application, a final overall impact score will be given by each of the two review committee members, reflecting their evaluation of the overall impact of the project in its entirety. The final impact score for each discussed application will be determined by calculating the arithmetic average of all the individual committee members’ impact scores.

      OVERALL IMPACT SCORE
      Impact Score Descriptor Additional Guidance
      High 9 Exceptional Exceptionally strong program with essentially no weaknesses.
      8 Outstanding Extremely strong program with negligible weaknesses or side effects
      7 Excellent Very strong program with only some minor weaknesses or side effects
      Medium 6 Very Good Strong program but with numerous minor weaknesses or side effects
      5 Good Strong program but with at least one moderate weakness or side effect
      4 Satisfactory Some strengths but also some moderate weaknesses or side effects
      Low 3 Fair Some strengths but with at least one major weakness or side effect
      2 Marginal May have few strengths and a few major weaknesses and/or side effects
      1 Poor Very few strengths and numerous major weaknesses and/or side effects
    3. Evaluation Criteria summary (see separate Scoring Matrix for details)

      4. Overall Impact. Reviewers will provide an overall impact score to reflect their assessment of the likelihood for the rural development program to exert a sustained, powerful influence on the lives of the villagers and the needs of that village, in consideration of the core review criteria listed below.

      Core Review Criteria. Reviewers will consider each of the review criteria below in determining the merit and overall impact of the rural development program. An application does not need to be strong in all categories to be judged likely to have major impact. For example, a program by its very nature may be innovative and appear essential to advance a field.

      Best Practice Evaluation Criteria, include the following (incl weighing factors as shown below):

      Implementation success (vs original goals)   10%
      Impact assessment (eg.# of people affected, depth of impact); independent report if available 15%
      Scalability or Scaling potential – business model, funding needed, ROIC 15%
      Use of appropriate technology; innovation in approach/solution 10%
      Collaboration partners, involvement of stakeholders, Govt ministries, building an ecosystem 10%
      Quality of funding/support; Viability & sustainability 10%
      Enabling Skills development; co-creating and local empowerment 10%
      Integration with holistic solution; alignment with national priorities 10%
      Feedback from recipients, end users, stakeholders 5%
      Lessons learned, any unintended consequences, or side effects 5%

Speaker Framework

  • Keynote addresses by Global leaders: PMO, UN SDG, etc (30,000ft view)
  • Invited speakers to address various points-of view (15,000ft view):
    • Central government priorities - Ministry of Rural Development & Panchayati Raj
    • State Government - State Chief Ministers
    • Local Governments - Panchayat Members panel (Mission Samriddhi led)
    • Academic/Research Institute: IITs, Stanford (Directors panel)
    • Industry CSR initiative: CSR Panel
  • Speakers from practitioner (Ground level view)
    • NGOs from each field (Parallel sessions): From a global competition of Best Practices, winners in each of the Tracks will be invited to present in their respective sessions:
      • Agriculture & Farming
      • Water & Sanitation
      • Health & Nutrition
      • Energy & Environment
      • Education & Skilling
      • Livelihood & Sustainability
    • Best Practices Awards - Special VIP Invitees (Corporate / Govt )

WGF Conference will be held in India in view of the Indian Government’s major push in rural development. This is to initiate a global movement in Integrated Rural Development.

Given the high quality and diversity of topics, speakers, organizations and participants over the past six years, the theme for 2022 Ideal Village Conference will be “Rural Transformation for Sustainable Growth” to capture the lessons learnt and best practices from successful implementations and thereby define the future of global rural development.
